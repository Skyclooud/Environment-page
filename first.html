WATER POLLUTION

Water pollution refers to the contamination of bodies of water, such as rivers, lakes, oceans, and groundwater, with harmful substances. These substances can come from a variety of sources, including industrial and agricultural runoff, sewage, and waste disposal. The effects of water pollution can be devastating, both for human health and the environment. It can lead to the spread of waterborne diseases, the destruction of aquatic ecosystems, and the loss of plant and animal species..

NOISE POLLUTION

Noise pollution refers to harmful substances present in the air that can have negative effects on human health and the environment. Common sources include industry, transportation, and natural sources like wildfires. It can cause respiratory and other health problems, harm the environment, and contribute to climate change. To reduce air pollution, individuals can use public transportation, carpool, and reduce energy consumption, while governments can implement regulations to limit emissions and promote renewable energy sources.
